Parsa Poorsistani

Undergraduate Student at AUT

parsa(dot)ps(at)aut(dot)ac(dot)ir

I recently completed my BSc in Computer Science at Amirkabir University of Technology. Currently, I work as a Software Engineer at Cafe Bazaar, where I build video streaming and analytics infrastructure.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked on several research projects:

  • At the University of Melbourne, I worked with Prof. Adel N. Toosi on GraphFlash, a serverless framework for graph processing, currently under review.
  • I contributed to SpotProxy, a censorship circumvention system featured at USENIX Security '24, under Prof. Ang Chen at the University of Michigan.
  • At CloudComLab, I worked with Prof. Seyyed Ahmad Javadi on latency-aware scheduling for Kubernetes, resulting in "LEAD: Latency-Efficient Application Deployment for Microservices Architecture", featured at ICAC '24, where I served as co-first author.

My research interests focus on systems, with particular emphasis on Operating Systems, Distributed Systems, and Networked Systems.
